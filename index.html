Why DNS Security Matters


As organizations move forward with digitalization, internet security has become more important than ever.

As organizations take major steps toward digital transformation, internet security has become more important than ever. Employees access company resources, accounting and finance software, ERP, SAP, CRM, and other critical systems over the internet.

However, during this process, the number of cyber threats faced by organizations is rapidly increasing. These threats bring risks such as data breaches, system disruptions, and the compromise of corporate networks, laboratory environments, and user data through malicious software.

Providing a secure and uninterrupted digital experience is no longer a luxury it is a necessity.

DNS-Based Cyber Threats

DNS plays a vital role in managing internet traffic and unfortunately becomes a target for many cyber attacks. If DNS attacks are not prevented, they put your network security and personal data at risk.

DNS Tunneling Attacks

Cybercriminals use DNS tunneling to transport malicious data and enable data theft. This technique exploits DNS to exfiltrate sensitive information from your network.

DNS Poisoning

DNS poisoning redirects users to fraudulent websites to steal credentials. Users are deceived into visiting fake sites that appear legitimate.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks lure users to deceptive websites to obtain personal information. These targeted attacks often appear to come from trusted sources.

Botnet Command & Control

Compromised machines communicate with command and control servers. DNSFEND blocks these communications before they can cause damage.

Malware Distribution

Malware-hosting servers use DNS to deliver infections. We block access to known malware distribution points.

Ransomware Attacks

Ransomware uses DNS to contact command centers. DNSFEND prevents communication and stops infections early.

How DNSFEND Works
