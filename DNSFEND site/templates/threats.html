DNS-Based Cyber Threats

DNS plays a vital role in managing internet traffic and unfortunately becomes a target for many cyberattacks. Understanding these threats is the first step toward protection.

Common DNS Attack Vectors

If DNS attacks are not prevented, they put your network security and personal data at risk. Here are the most common threats organizations face.

DNS Tunneling

Cybercriminals use DNS tunneling to transport malicious data and enable data theft. This technique exploits DNS to exfiltrate sensitive information by encoding data within DNS queries and responses.

DNS Poisoning

DNS poisoning (cache poisoning) redirects users to fraudulent websites to steal credentials. Users are deceived into visiting fake sites that appear legitimate, leading to credential theft.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks lure users to deceptive websites to obtain personal information. These targeted attacks often appear to come from trusted sources, making them difficult to detect.

Botnet Command & Control

Compromised machines communicate with command and control servers via DNS. These botnets can be used for DDoS attacks, spam distribution, and large-scale data theft.

Malware Distribution

Malware-hosting servers use DNS to deliver infections. Attackers register malicious domains to distribute trojans, spyware, and other harmful software to unsuspecting users.

Ransomware Attacks

Ransomware uses DNS to contact command centers, download encryption keys, and report successful infections. Blocking DNS communication can stop ransomware in its tracks.
